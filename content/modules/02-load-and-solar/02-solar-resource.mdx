## Two ways to talk about sunlight

- **GHI (Global Horizontal Irradiance)** — instantaneous solar power on a horizontal surface, in **W/m²**. Peaks near noon at ~1000 W/m² on a clear day.
- **PSH (Peak Sun Hours)** — the equivalent number of hours of 1000 W/m² that would deliver the same daily energy. PSH is GHI integrated over the day and divided by 1000.

PSH is the number you actually use for sizing. It collapses an entire day's irradiance into one
multiplier:

> **Daily PV energy (Wh) ≈ Array Watts × PSH × derating**

## Regional values to start from

These are reasonable starting numbers for Nigeria, before you replace them with site data:

| Zone | Typical PSH (annual avg) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Far north (Sokoto, Katsina, Yobe) | **5.8–6.2** | Highest. Dust and harmattan haze reduce dry-season output. |
| Middle belt (Abuja, Jos, Kaduna) | **5.0–5.5** | Strong, with afternoon cloud build-up in the rainy season. |
| South-west (Lagos, Ibadan, Akure) | **4.5–5.0** | Heavier wet-season cloud. |
| South-east / Niger Delta | **4.0–4.5** | Wettest. Plan for the long rainy season. |

<RegulationAlert>
These are rule-of-thumb numbers for early screening only. For a real project, pull
satellite-derived data (NASA POWER, Solargis, or a paid resource assessment) for the specific
coordinates of your site.
</RegulationAlert>

## Derating: why nameplate watts lie

A 500 Wp module rarely produces 500 W in the field. Real-world derating stacks:

- **Temperature** — modules lose ~0.35–0.45 %/°C above 25 °C. In Nigerian sun a module surface can hit 55–65 °C → 10–15 % loss alone.
- **Soiling** — dust and harmattan film. 3–8 % unless cleaned.
- **Wiring and mismatch** — 2–4 %.
- **Inverter and battery round-trip** — 5–15 %.

A combined **0.75 derating** is conservative and common for early sizing. Refine when you have
component-level data.

## What the model needs (and where to source it)

| Category | Specifics | Where it comes from |
|---|---|---|
| **Load profile** | Hourly kWh × 365 days | Your Load Survey Lab artifact + a coincidence/seasonal factor pass |
| **Solar resource** | Monthly GHI, ambient temperature | NASA POWER, Solargis (preferred for bankability), or paid resource report |
| **PV array** | Tech, rating, derating, tilt, azimuth | Module datasheet + site visit |
| **Battery** | Chemistry, capacity, round-trip efficiency, DoD, cycle life, calendar life | Datasheet + warranty terms |
| **Genset** | Rated kW, fuel curve (L/kWh at part loads), minimum load fraction, replacement interval | Manufacturer + local procurement quote |
| **Inverter** | Rating, efficiency curve, lifetime | Datasheet |
| **Economics** | Component costs, O&M, fuel price, replacement schedules, discount rate, project life | Quotes + grant terms + market |
| **Dispatch & control** | Strategy, setpoints | Designer's choice — defend it |
| **Reliability target** | Maximum unmet load %, autonomy | Project requirement — usually 1–5 % unmet for rural Nigerian sites |

## Defensible Nigerian defaults when you're missing data

These are starting points, not final values. Replace with real data as it arrives.

- **PSH:** 5.5 north / 5.0 middle belt / 4.5 south (rainy-season conservative).
- **Ambient temperature:** 28 °C average; 32 °C design ambient for derating.
- **Derating (PV → DC bus):** 0.78–0.82 depending on shading.
- **Lead-acid battery round-trip:** 0.80 with DoD 0.5; **LFP:** 0.95 with DoD 0.8.
- **Genset fuel:** start with 0.30 L/kWh at full load; rises sharply below 30 % load.
- **Diesel price:** sensitivity ±30 % around the day-of-modelling price.
- **Discount rate:** 12–15 % for private Nigerian developers; lower if blended with DFI debt.
- **Project life:** 20 years (PV); battery replacement at year 8–10 (LFP) or 5–7 (lead-acid).

<RegulationAlert>
Fuel-price sensitivity is no longer optional. Nigerian diesel prices moved more than 100 % in
recent years. Run your simulation with at least three fuel scenarios and report all three.
</RegulationAlert>

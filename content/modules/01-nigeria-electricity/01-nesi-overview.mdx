## The path electricity takes

Power in Nigeria flows through three layers, each owned by a different kind of company:

1. **Generation (GenCos)** — power stations, mostly gas-fired, some hydro, some emerging solar.
2. **Transmission (TCN)** — the high-voltage backbone, **330 kV** for long distances and **132 kV** for regional transfer. Single state-owned company.
3. **Distribution (DisCos)** — eleven regional licensees that step voltage down to **33 kV**, **11 kV**, and **low voltage** for the customer's meter.

<Callout tone="info">
Voltage drops as power gets closer to the customer because long-distance transmission needs
high voltage to keep losses low, but high voltage can't be safely used in homes and shops.
</Callout>

## Where the gaps are

Two kinds of customer suffer the most:

- **Unserved**: communities the grid simply has not reached.
- **Underserved**: communities connected on paper but with so few hours of supply per day that
  the connection is not useful — many DisCo customers in lower service bands sit here.

This is the gap mini-grids exist to close.

<EvidenceQuote lessonId="lesson-1-01" source="Course content brief, Nigerian regulatory context">
Nigeria has unserved and underserved communities where mini-grids support electricity access.
Mini-grids serve households, MSMEs, agro-processing, cold storage, irrigation, public
facilities, and productive use.
</EvidenceQuote>

## Who decides what

| Actor | Role |
|---|---|
| **NERC** | Federal regulator. Issues mini-grid permits at scale; sets the framework. |
| **SERCs** | State regulators. Where they're operational, they have primary authority for *intrastate* electricity. |
| **REA** | Rural Electrification Agency. Runs grant programmes (e.g. DARES). |
| **NEMSA** | Inspects electrical installations, meters, and instruments. |
| **SON** | Standards body. Issues SONCAP (imports) and MANCAP (local manufacture). |
| **COREN** | Engineering registration body. Designs above a threshold need a COREN-signed engineer. |

<RegulationAlert>
The 2026 NERC Mini-Grid Regulations updated the thresholds: isolated mini-grids up to **5 MW**
per site, interconnected up to **10 MW** per site. Projects up to **100 kW** can register;
larger ones need a permit.
</RegulationAlert>

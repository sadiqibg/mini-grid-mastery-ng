A mini-grid is *like* a tiny utility. The analogy is useful — and incomplete. Use the analogy
to get oriented; notice where it leaks before you trust it.

## Where the "tiny utility" analogy leaks

- A national utility cross-subsidises rural service with urban revenues. A mini-grid can't.
- A national utility can absorb a single bad year. A mini-grid can collapse if collections drop for two months.
- A national utility plans on decades. A mini-grid developer must show payback in a few years to be financeable.

These differences are why **community engagement, tariff design, and O&M discipline** matter more in mini-grids than in any other part of the power sector.

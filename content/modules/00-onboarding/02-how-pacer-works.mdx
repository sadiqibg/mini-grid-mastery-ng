Reading is not the same as learning. PACER is the method this site uses to turn each kind of
content into the kind of *practice* that actually builds skill.

| Type | What it is | What you'll do here |
|---|---|---|
| **Procedural** | Step-by-step methods | Practice in a lab |
| **Analogous** | Things that look alike but aren't | Critique trade-offs |
| **Conceptual** | Networks of ideas | Build your own concept map |
| **Evidence** | Facts, data, thresholds | Store as proof for later |
| **Reference** | Vocabulary | Spaced-repetition flashcards |

<Callout tone="info">
Lessons are tagged with their primary PACER type. The digestion task you see at the end of
each lesson — open the lab, build the map, drill the deck — is what turns reading into a skill.
</Callout>

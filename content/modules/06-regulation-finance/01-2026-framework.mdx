## The thresholds you must know

<RegulationAlert>
The 2026 Mini-Grid Regulations supersede the 2016/2017 thresholds. If a document or trainer
still quotes 1 MW as the limit, they are out of date.
</RegulationAlert>

| Setting | 2026 value |
|---|---|
| Isolated mini-grid, max per site | **5 MW** |
| Interconnected mini-grid, max per site | **10 MW** |
| Registration threshold (no permit needed) | **up to 100 kW** |
| Permit pathway | **>100 kW** |
| NERC application response (target) | **30 business days** |
| DisCo silence rule for interconnected | **15 business days** → silence = no objection |
| Tripartite Agreement | **Required for interconnected mini-grids** (developer + DisCo + community) |

## Three jurisdictional rules

1. **Federal vs state** — NERC has authority over interstate and grid-linked cases. **SERCs** have primary authority over **intrastate** electricity systems in states where they are operational.
2. **Determine jurisdiction first.** Pick the right regulator before you draft anything. Project type (isolated/interconnected), capacity, and state determine the answer.
3. **Both regimes use the same technical, safety, and community-engagement standards.** Jurisdiction changes who you submit to, not what compliance looks like.

## The actor map you'll meet on every project

- **NERC / SERC** — issue the permit or accept the registration.
- **DisCo** — must be notified and engaged for interconnected projects; has 15 business days to object.
- **REA** — runs grant programmes (e.g. DARES); not a regulator.
- **NEMSA** — inspects electrical installations, meters, and instruments before energisation.
- **SON** — runs **SONCAP** for regulated imports and **MANCAP** for local manufacture.
- **COREN** — engineering registration; designs above the threshold need a COREN-signed engineer.
- **NESREA** — environmental compliance; relevant for larger sites and sensitive land use.
- **The community** — host community agreement (HCA), willingness to pay, land rights.

<NigerianExample>
A 750 kW isolated solar-battery-diesel project in a state with an operational SERC is intrastate
and above the registration threshold → SERC permit, NEMSA inspection, SONCAP-verified imports
for regulated components, COREN-signed design, HCA on file. A 2 MW interconnected project in
the same state is grid-linked → NERC permit, Tripartite Agreement required, DisCo notification
with the 15-day clock running.
</NigerianExample>

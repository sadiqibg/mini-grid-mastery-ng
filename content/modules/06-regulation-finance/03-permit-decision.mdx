## The decision tree, in one page

Ask these four questions in order. Each answer narrows the pathway.

1. **Is the project isolated or interconnected with the DisCo network?**
   - Isolated → next question
   - Interconnected → Tripartite Agreement + NERC permit (the DisCo also has 15 business days)

2. **What is the installed capacity?**
   - ≤ 100 kW → registration pathway
   - 100 kW – 5 MW (isolated) or up to 10 MW (interconnected) → permit pathway

3. **Is the project intrastate, and does the state have an operational SERC?**
   - Yes → SERC has primary authority
   - No → NERC

4. **Is the design above the COREN threshold?**
   - Yes → COREN-registered engineer must sign the design

## What's always required (regardless of pathway)

- **NEMSA inspection** before energisation.
- **SONCAP** on regulated imports; **MANCAP** on locally manufactured regulated components.
- **Host Community Agreement** documenting land rights, dispute resolution, and revenue-sharing where applicable.
- **As-built drawings** filed with the regulator and kept on site.

<Callout tone="info">
"NERC vs SERC" is not a matter of preference. It's determined by jurisdiction. Confirm before
filing — re-routing a submission costs weeks.
</Callout>

## Five categories, not one

Many early-stage developers build a BoQ with three line items: PV modules, batteries, inverter.
That is the **generation block** only. A real hybrid BoQ has **five categories**:

| Category | Examples of line items |
|---|---|
| **Generation** | PV modules, mounting structures, inverters, charge controllers, genset, fuel tank |
| **Storage** | Battery bank, BMS, battery enclosure, cooling |
| **Distribution & power management** | LV cables, poles, drop-cables, distribution box, protection devices, ATS, surge protection, meters, load limiters |
| **Civil works & installation** | Control room, foundation, fencing, signage, conduit, earth electrodes, labour |
| **General items** | Logistics, commissioning, spares, safety equipment (PPE), labels, training, as-built drawings, documentation |

## Where projects fall over

<EvidenceQuote lessonId="lesson-5-02" source="Course doc §9 — Module 5 BoQ warnings">
Incomplete BoQs focused only on PV modules, batteries, and inverters are the leading cause of
project cost overruns. A complete hybrid BoQ includes generation, storage, distribution,
civil works, installation, commissioning, logistics, safety equipment, labels, signage, and
as-built drawings.
</EvidenceQuote>

Three categories developers routinely under-spec or skip:

- **Surge protection and earthing.** Outdoor sites in Nigeria see meaningful lightning activity. SPDs and a proper earth electrode array are not optional. They are also cheap if specified up-front and expensive if added after a strike.
- **Spares.** Without on-site spare fuses, MCBs, RCDs, and at least one spare inverter card, a single fault can mean weeks of downtime. Spares belong in the BoQ.
- **As-built drawings and documentation.** Required for NEMSA inspection and for the next engineer who has to maintain the site three years from now.

<Callout tone="warning">
If a BoQ does not include logistics, signage, commissioning, and training, it is incomplete.
Ask for the missing categories before approving spend.
</Callout>

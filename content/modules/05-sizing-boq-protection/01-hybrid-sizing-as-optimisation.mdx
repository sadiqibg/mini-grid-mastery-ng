Sizing PV, then sizing the battery, then sizing the generator, then sizing the inverter as if
each one was independent is the most common amateur mistake. It produces:

- Oversized PV that you can't absorb or store on bright days
- Undersized battery that gets cycled to death by the genset
- Oversized genset that runs at low load → poor fuel efficiency → wears out
- An inverter that's right for the average day and wrong for the worst day

Hybrid sizing is **one optimisation problem** over the **same load profile**, the **same PSH**,
the **same fuel cost**, and the **same dispatch strategy**.

## Three dispatch strategies you'll meet

| Strategy | What it does | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Load following** | Genset matches load; battery and PV fill gaps | High variable load, expensive fuel |
| **Cycle charging** | Genset runs at high load and tops up battery | Predictable load, want fewer genset starts |
| **Combined** | Switches between the two based on time of day | Real Nigerian sites — most of them |

Dispatch is **not** a setting you pick last. It feeds back into how much battery and how much
PV you actually need. A cycle-charging design with a small battery is a different system from
a load-following design with a big one, even when the topology is identical.

<Callout tone="warning">
"We'll sort dispatch out at commissioning" is a red flag in any project plan. Dispatch belongs
in sizing.
</Callout>

## Where the standalone analogy fails

You learned the formula `PV = Demand / (PSH × derating)` in Module 3. Don't use it for hybrid:

- It assumes PV is the only generation source. It isn't.
- It ignores the genset's contribution on the worst week.
- It ignores curtailment on the best week (PV you can't use).
- It implies one right answer per demand number. There are infinitely many sizing pairs that
  meet a hybrid LCoE or reliability target; the optimiser finds the cheapest.

## What the optimiser is actually doing

It searches over `(PV size, battery size, genset size, dispatch)` and reports each candidate's:

- **Net Present Cost (NPC)** — total lifetime cost discounted to today
- **LCoE** — total cost ÷ total energy served
- **Unmet load** — fraction of demand never delivered
- **Renewable fraction** — share of energy from PV vs genset
- **Fuel consumed** — annual litres of diesel

Module 8 walks you through preparing the inputs for HOMER Pro or PVSyst. For now, internalise
that **sizing is search, not arithmetic**.
